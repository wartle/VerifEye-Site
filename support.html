Frequently Asked Questions

What location data does VerifEye add to my captures?

VerifEye can overlay GPS coordinates, a reverse-geocoded address, a map thumbnail, date and time, altitude, barometric pressure, current weather, GPS accuracy, Plus Code, timezone, and a custom note or hashtag — on both photos and videos. You choose exactly which data appears.

Can I customize how the overlay looks?

Yes. You can choose between a clean strip below the photo or a floating overlay panel. You can also pick which data fields to include, select coordinate formats (decimal, degrees & minutes, DMS, or Plus Codes), and choose date/time formats. You can even add your own logo in any corner.

Can I record video with the location overlay?

Yes. VerifEye records video with the live GPS overlay burned directly into the footage, and it logs the full location track for the clip — exportable as GPX, CSV, or KML. You can also choose a video stabilization mode and capture in HDR.

What do the 3D Scan and 360° features do?

On supported iPhones, VerifEye captures 3D scans — LiDAR room scans or photogrammetry object scans — with floor-area and volume metrics and USDZ / PLY export. The 360° PhotoSphere mode stitches a full panorama you can explore by drag or gyro, and export as a geotagged Tiny Planet.

Does VerifEye upload my photos to the cloud?

Capture and overlay compositing happen entirely on your device, and there are no accounts or sign-ins. On iPhone and iPad, cloud sync is an optional, off-by-default feature that syncs your library through your own private iCloud Drive — never our servers. The Android version keeps everything on the device. See our Privacy Policy for the full detail.

What GPS coordinate formats are supported?

VerifEye supports four formats: decimal degrees (e.g., 10.3186, 123.9057), degrees and decimal minutes, full DMS notation (degrees, minutes, seconds), and Plus Codes for universal location sharing.

Which devices are supported?

VerifEye runs on iPhone (App Store) and Android (Google Play). Multi-lens features (ultra-wide, telephoto) need a device that has those lenses, and LiDAR room scanning requires a Pro iPhone with a LiDAR sensor.

Is VerifEye free? What does Pro unlock?

The core geotag photo camera is free. Premium capabilities — GPS video recording, 3D / LiDAR scanning, 360° photos, iCloud sync (iOS), and offline maps — are available through a Pro subscription, and several modules can also be unlocked individually as one-time purchases.

How do I add my company logo?

In the overlay settings, you can select an image from your photo library to use as a logo or branding mark. You can position it in any corner of the photo.

Can I organize my captures into albums?

Yes. Photos, videos, and 3D scans share one unified gallery, and you can create albums to keep projects, job sites, or assignments separate. Browse in a grid, multi-select, share in bulk, or open any location directly in Maps.

How accurate is the GPS data?

VerifEye uses your iPhone's built-in GPS hardware. Accuracy depends on your environment — typically within a few meters outdoors. The GPS accuracy value is displayed on the overlay so you can verify precision for each shot.
